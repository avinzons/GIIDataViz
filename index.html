What is Global Innovation Index?

The Global Innovation Index (GII) aims to capture the multi-dimensional facets of innovation and provide the tools that can assist in tailoring policies to promote long-term output growth, improved productivity, and job growth.

How is GII Calculated?

The GII is a weighted average of seven different sub-indexes:

Institutions Human Capital and Research Infrastructure Market Sophistication
Government effectiveness, regulatory quality, ease of starting a business, ease of paying taxes, etc. Expenditure on education, terciary education enrollment, gross expenditure on R&D, graduates in science and engineering, etc. Information and communication technologies (ICTs) access, electricity output, logistics performance, GDP per unit of energy use, etc. Credit access, ease of getting credit, venture capital deals, applied tariff rate, intensity of local competition, etc.
Business Sophistication Knowledge and Technology Outputs Creative Outputs
Females employed with advanced degrees, university/industry research collaboration, joint ventures and strategic alliances, foreign direct investment, etc. Scientific and technical publications, patent applications, ISO 9001 quality certificates, total computer software spending, etc. ICTs and business model creation, contribution to global entertainment and media market, generic top-level domains(gTLDs), Wikipedia yearly edits, video uploads on YouTube, etc.

The political regime data ranges on a scale from -10 (full autocracy) to 10 (full democracy). Democracies are distinct from autocratic countries in which political preferences cannot be expressed and citizens are not guaranteed civil liberties. Anocracies – a term used often in this entry – are regimes that fall in the middle of the spectrum of autocracies and democracies. Anocracies are countries which are not fully autocratic, but which can also not be called democratic.
